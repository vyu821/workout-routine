TipsSuperset laterals with front raises to save time. Flat and incline press are your heaviest lifts so do those first. If short on time, drop one of the finisher push-up sets.

Dumbbell Flat Bench Press

90s rest
Primary chest mass builder. Control the descent, pause briefly at the chest, drive up. Keep your shoulder blades pinched back into the bench throughout.
Incline Dumbbell Press
Pec major (clavicular)
3 × 10–12
75s rest
Set bench to 30–45°. Hits the upper chest that flat work misses. Keep elbows at about 70° to the torso, not flared wide.
Dumbbell Chest Fly
Pec major (stretch)Pec minor
3 × 12–15
60s rest
Flat bench. Arms slightly bent, lower wide until you feel a deep stretch, squeeze back together. Isolation move, go lighter than you think.

60s rest
Finisher. Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Go to failure or do slow negatives (3 sec down). Great for serratus anterior and pec minor activation.
Shoulders

4 × 8–10
90s rest
Bench fully upright. Shoulder mass builder. Press to full extension, lower to ear level. Core tight throughout.
Superset
Dumbbell Lateral Raise
Medial delt
4 × 12–15
no rest
Lead with the elbow, slight forward tilt, raise to shoulder height. Avoid shrugging. Slow and controlled, not swung.
Dumbbell Front Raise
Anterior delt
3 × 12
60s rest
Alternating or both arms. Raise to shoulder height, no higher. Slight bend in elbow. Rounds out the front delt that pressing alone doesn't fully isolate.
Triceps

Long headMedial head
3 × 10–12
75s rest
Flat bench. Lower dumbbells to the sides of your head, elbows pointing up. The long head gets the best stretch in this position. Key for overall tricep size.
Overhead DB Extension
Long head (stretch)
3 × 12
60s rest
One dumbbell held with both hands overhead. Lower behind your head, elbows in tight. The stretch-loaded position on the long head is unmatched for growth.
Diamond / Close-Grip Push-Ups

2 × Fail
60s rest
Hands form a diamond or stay close. Elbows track back along your torso, not flared. Great finisher that hits the lateral head differently than DB work.
Pull
Focus
Back · Biceps · Forearms · Rear Delts
~55 min
TipsYou don't need all the bicep variations every session. Rotate: one session do curls + incline curls, another do Zottmans + concentration curls. Always superset the two wrist curl variations back to back.
Back
Dumbbell Row
Latissimus dorsiMid traps
4 × 8–10
90s rest
Brace a knee and hand on the bench. Row the dumbbell to your hip, leading with the elbow. Full stretch at the bottom, full squeeze at the top.
Pull-Ups / Chin-Ups
CalisthenicsLats (width)Biceps
3 × Fail
90s rest
Pull-ups (overhand) bias lats more; chin-ups (underhand) bring in more biceps. Go to failure every set. Even 3–5 reps per set adds up.
Incline Prone DB Row
Mid / lower trapsRhomboids
3 × 12
75s rest
Set bench to 45°, lie chest-down, dumbbells hanging. Row both up, elbows flared to 90°. Excellent for postural muscles and upper back thickness standard rows miss.
Dumbbell Deadlift
Erector spinaeLower back
3 × 10
90s rest
Dumbbells at your sides, hip hinge (not a squat), flat back throughout. Push the floor away on the way up. Builds spinal erectors and teaches the hinge pattern.
Biceps
Dumbbell Curl
Biceps brachii (short head)
3 × 10–12
60s rest
Standing, alternating. Supinate (rotate) the wrist as you curl up. Full range of motion, no swinging.
Incline Dumbbell Curl
Biceps brachii (long head)
3 × 10–12
60s rest
Bench at 45–60°, lean back, arms hang fully behind you. Puts the long head in a deep stretch standing curls can't replicate.
Hammer Curl
BrachialisBrachioradialis
3 × 12
60s rest
Neutral (thumbs-up) grip throughout. The brachialis sits beneath the bicep and, when developed, pushes the bicep up making arms look thicker.
Concentration Curl
Biceps (short head)Peak isolation
2 × 12 /side
45s rest
Seated, elbow braced against the inside of your thigh, curl up. Eliminates all momentum. Best used as a finisher after heavier curl work.
Forearms
Zottman Curl
BrachioradialisBiceps brachiiBrachialis
3 × 10
60s rest
Curl up supinated (palms up), rotate to pronated (palms down) at the top, lower slowly. Trains all three elbow flexors through their strongest positions in one movement.
Reverse Curl
BrachioradialisExtensor carpi
3 × 10–12
60s rest
Overhand (pronated) grip, curl up the same as a normal curl. Brachioradialis does most of the work. Also hits the wrist extensors.
Superset
Wrist Curl (Supinated)
Flexor carpi radialisFlexor carpi ulnaris
3 × 15–20
no rest
Forearms on thighs or bench edge, palms up. Curl the wrist up and lower slowly. Builds the inner forearm belly.
Wrist Curl (Pronated)
Extensor carpi radialisExtensor carpi ulnaris
3 × 15–20
30s rest
Same setup but palms down. Extend the wrist up against gravity. The most neglected forearm muscles, and a common source of lateral elbow pain when weak.
Rear Delts
Bent-Over Reverse Fly
Posterior deltLower traps
4 × 12–15
60s rest
Hinge forward to ~45°, arms hanging. Raise arms out to the sides with a slight bend at the elbow, pinch the shoulder blades. Rear delts are almost always undertrained and matter for shoulder health and posture.
Face Pull (floor variation)
Posterior deltRotator cuff
3 × 15
60s rest
Lie face-down on the bench with light dumbbells, arms hanging. Row the weights up and back, externally rotating at the top. Crucial for shoulder health and rotator cuff longevity.

TipsDo the full warmup before touching a weight. Keep the heaviest compounds first: goblet squat, then RDL. Superset standing and seated calf raises back to back, then do tibialis raises after.

Sit with both knees bent at 90°, one in front and one to the side. Sit tall and breathe into the stretch. Opens the hip capsule in a way standing stretches don't reach.

Rear foot elevated on the bench, dumbbells at sides. Lower the back knee toward the floor. Brutal but one of the best single-leg exercises you can do.

75s rest
Wide stance, squat deeply to one side while the other leg stays fully extended with toes pointing up. Hold a dumbbell at chest for counterbalance.
